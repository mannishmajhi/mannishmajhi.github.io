Manish Majhi

In the dance of cosmos, where dualities entwine,
Shiva rests as consciousness, the vast, the divine.
Yet without Shakti, the pulse of his might,
He remains lifeless, like the darkness of the night.

Narayana, the body, the animate, the form,
Yet without Laxmi, there’s no motion, no storm.
She fuels every action, each breath and each beat,
Making life’s journey rich and complete.

From Narayana’s navel, the brain takes its root,
Brahma, the creator, in wisdom absolute.
Saraswati as the knowledge, pure and true,
Gives purpose to the mind, guiding what it must do.

Thus, the Deva, lifeless, without Devi’s embrace,
And the Devi, inert, without her Deva’s presence.
Together they thrive, in unison they dwell,
For one without the other is an existence in vain.
